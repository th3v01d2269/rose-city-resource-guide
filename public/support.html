📋 Government & Foundation Grant Opportunities

This project aligns well with several federal and foundation funding streams. Most require nonprofit (501c3) status or a fiscal sponsor. The Oregon Community Foundation, Community Action Agencies, or a local nonprofit could serve as fiscal sponsors.

HHS / ACF Social Services Technology Grant Rolling Applications

The Administration for Children and Families funds technology projects that improve access to social services for low-income populations. This guide — connecting unhoused people, vehicle dwellers, and low-income families to 2,088 free resources — aligns directly with ACF's mission.

Apply at: grants.gov · Search: "ACF social services technology" · Agency: HHS/ACF

Typical award: $50K–$500K · Requires: 501c3 or fiscal sponsor, SAM.gov registration, UEI number

SAMHSA Homelessness Prevention Technology Annual NOFO

SAMHSA funds projects addressing mental health, substance use, and homelessness. A resource guide that connects people to mental health, recovery, and safe parking resources fits their Continuum of Care priorities.

Apply at: samhsa.gov/grants · Search: "PATH Program" or "Continuum of Care"

Typical award: $100K–$1M · Requires: 501c3, SAM.gov, matching funds often required

HUD Continuum of Care (CoC) Technology Grant Annual — Fall NOFO

HUD's CoC program funds local homelessness response systems. A centralized resource directory with safe parking, shelter, and housing navigation serves CoC goals. Apply through your local Continuum of Care (Oregon CoC contact: Community Connect 503-640-3263).

Apply at: hud.gov/program_offices/comm_planning/coc · Local CoC: Community Connect

Typical award: $50K–$2M · Requires: Apply through local CoC lead agency

USDA Rural Development Community Facilities Grant Rolling Applications

USDA funds essential community facilities in rural areas — including public health and safety services. This guide serves rural Oregon and all 50 states, making it eligible for rural technology infrastructure grants.

Apply at: rd.usda.gov/programs-services/community-facilities/community-facilities-direct-loan-grant-program

Typical award: Up to $50K for technology

Oregon Community Foundation — Technology & Innovation Rolling

OCF funds Oregon nonprofits improving social services. A resource guide specific to Oregon with 600+ local resources is a strong candidate. OCF also provides fiscal sponsorship options for projects without their own 501c3.

Apply at: oregoncf.org/grants · Contact: grants@oregoncf.org

Typical award: $5K–$50K for technology projects

Meyer Memorial Trust — Oregon Social Services Rolling

Oregon's largest private foundation — funds projects serving low-income Oregonians. A free public resource directory with 600+ Oregon resources aligns with their housing stability and economic opportunity priorities.

Apply at: mmt.org/grants · Portland-based foundation, Oregon focus

Typical award: $25K–$250K

Ford Foundation — Technology for Social Justice Competitive

Ford funds technology projects that advance equity and economic opportunity. A community-built resource guide for unhoused and low-income populations that is open-source and community-submitted fits their digital equity focus.

Apply at: fordfoundation.org/work/challenging-inequality · Requires 501c3

Typical award: $100K–$1M

📝 How to Apply: Step-by-Step

1. Register on SAM.gov — Every federal grant requires a Unique Entity Identifier (UEI). Free at sam.gov. Takes 1-2 weeks.

2. Get a fiscal sponsor — Without a 501c3, partner with Community Action (503-648-6646), OCF, or Oregon Nonprofit Association for fiscal sponsorship. They handle compliance for a small fee (~5-8%).

3. Register on grants.gov — Federal grant portal. Free. Required for HHS, SAMHSA, HUD, and USDA applications.

4. Document impact — Track monthly: page views, unique users, resources searched, community submissions. Grant applications need data.

5. Write a one-page summary — "2,088 free resources, 50 states, 0 ads, open-source. Serving people experiencing homelessness, vehicle dwellers, and low-income families."
