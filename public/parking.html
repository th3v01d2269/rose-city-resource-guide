Verified Safe Parking Programs

These organizations specifically run or coordinate safe overnight parking for people living in vehicles. Free, non-judgmental, often with case management.

Before You Park
  • Always ask permission before overnight parking at private lots
  • Park in well-lit areas near other vehicles when possible
  • Know your local laws — some cities have vehicle habitation bans
  • Have a backup location in mind in case you're asked to leave
  • Keep your registration and ID accessible
  • Call 211 — they often know local safe parking programs not listed online
Safety While Sleeping
  • Use window covers for privacy — they also help with temperature regulation
  • Keep a phone charged. 911 works even without cell service
  • Don't run your engine for heat — carbon monoxide risk. Use blankets or a safe space heater
  • Keep doors locked and trust your instincts — move if something feels wrong
  • Keep a personal safety kit: water, food, flashlight, first aid, important documents
Your Rights
  • Police cannot search your vehicle without consent or a warrant (generally)
  • You do not have to tell officers where you live or what you're doing
  • Many cities have safe parking ordinances protecting vehicle dwellers
  • If harassed, document badge numbers and contact local legal aid
  • National Homelessness Law Center: nlchp.org — free legal resources
